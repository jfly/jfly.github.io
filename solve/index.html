Here are some notes on how I teach people to solve Rubik's Cube with a beginner's layer by layer method. There's no substitute for being taught in person, but if you're dedicated and patient, you can use the information on this page to solve Rubik's Cube.

Many tutorials optimize for minimal memorization but burden you with awkward pattern recognition and innefficient algorithms.

The method described here is largely inspired by what we taught at the Berkeley Rubik's Cube DeCal. Every algorithm here is used by world class speedcubers. After you learn this method, if you want to get faster, you won't have to unlearn anything.

Happy cubing!
Jeremy Fleischman (2005FLEI01)

Overview

The cube is made up of 3 types of pieces: corners, edgers, and centers. Corners have 3 stickers, edges have 2 stickers, and centers have 1 sticker.

The green-white-red corner.

Corners are unique. There is exactly 1 green-white-red corner.

The green-white edge.

Edges are also unique. There is exactly 1 green-white edge.

The white center.

Centers are also unique. There is exactly 1 white center.

A cube has 6 faces, but only 3 layers. The internal mechanism of a cube does not allow the centers to move (they can only spin in place), so we can describe faces by the color of their center piece.

On this cube, the white face is solved, but the white layer is not solved. Note how the green face has a mix of stickers along the top where there should just be green stickers. On this cube, the entire white face and layer are solved. Note how on the green, red, blue, and orange faces, there's a row of matching stickers along the top.

First Two Layers

First Two Layers is sometimes abbreviated as F2L. More advanced methods will actually tackle the first layer and the second layer simultaneously, but we're going to do it one layer at a time for now.

Next Steps

Congratulations on learning to solve Rubik's Cube! With this method and some practice, you should be able to solve under 60 seconds. If you're having trouble breaking the 1 minute barrier, focus on solving smoothly: turn slowly and try to eliminate pauses from your solves (it's hard to do!)

Once you're under a minute, start learning CFOP (learn intuitive F2L, and start learning some OLLs and PLLs). Go to a WCA competition, make some friends, and learn!
